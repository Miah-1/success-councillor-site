Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Q1: Can I get a government job through the councillor by submitting a complaint?

Submitting a complaint or request through this system does not guarantee a government job or employment. However, if appropriate, the councillor may provide a recommendation letter to support your application.

Q2: Can the local government fix major roads or highways?

Major roads and highways fall under the jurisdiction of the national government or relevant authorities, not the local government. You can report issues with major roads to the national transport agency.

Q3: Does the local government provide food cards or grants?

The local government does not directly provide food cards or financial grants. Such assistance programs are usually managed by social welfare agencies or national government departments.

Q4: Who can use this complaint management system?

This system is open to residents and community members within the councillor’s jurisdiction to submit complaints, report issues, or request assistance related to local governance.

Q5: How long will it take to get a response after submitting a complaint?

Response times may vary depending on the nature and complexity of the complaint. The councillor’s office aims to address submissions as promptly as possible.

Q6: What types of issues should I report using this system?

Use this system to report local issues such as garbage collection, street lighting, potholes on local roads, community safety concerns, and other municipal services.

Q7: What happens if my complaint is outside the local government’s responsibility?

Your complaint may be redirected to the appropriate authority, or you will be informed of the correct agency to contact.
